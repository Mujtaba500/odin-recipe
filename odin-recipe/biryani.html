Mutton Biryani

Description

Mutton biryani is a popular Indian dish made with tender pieces of goat meat cooked in aromatic spices and layered with fragrant, long-grain basmati rice. The meat is usually marinated in a blend of yogurt, ginger, garlic, and spices before being cooked with onions, tomatoes, and green chilies. The rice is also flavored with a mix of whole spices, saffron, and ghee to create a rich, complex flavor profile. This hearty and flavorful dish is often served with raita, a yogurt-based side dish, and papad, a crispy Indian flatbread.

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups of long-grain basmati rice
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup of plain yogurt 2 tbsp of ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp of red chili powder 1 tsp of turmeric powder 1 tsp of coriander powder 1/2 tsp of cumin powder 1/2 tsp of garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water a few times until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes and then drain it.
  2. In a bowl, mix the mutton pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and garam masala powder. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Heat the ghee or oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the cinnamon stick, green cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaf. Fry for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the sliced onion and saut√© until golden brown. Add the marinated mutton pieces and cook for about 10 minutes until the meat is slightly browned.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies and cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes are soft and pulpy.
  6. Add enough water to the pot to cook the rice. Add salt to taste. Bring to a boil.
  7. Add the soaked rice to the pot and stir gently. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes until the rice is cooked and the mutton is tender.
  8. Once the biryani is cooked, sprinkle the saffron milk on top of the rice and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve the mutton biryani hot with raita and papad.
